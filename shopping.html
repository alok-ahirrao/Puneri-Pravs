Shoppers Pune

Appa Balwant Chowk Pune (ABC PUNE)

Appa Balwant Chowk, conveniently called ABC, is for all book lovers. With wide price ranges, the stationary shops offer all you need to satisfy your stationery obsession. This place covers educational, fictional, non-fictional and all other categories of books. Shops here sell new, old and reused books and this place is everything a book lover needs. The Appa Balwant Chowk was named after Sardar Appa Balwant Mehendale, a commander in the Peshwa army. The place began to be known as a venue for booksellers in the late 1950s, following the opening of the first shop by L.N. Godbole. On August 9, 1942, two people were shot and killed by police at A.B.C. after a teenager raised the Indian flag in defiance of the British rule. That triggered anti-colonialist violence including a theatre bombing the 75th anniversary of which was marked in January 2018.

Fergusson College Road (F.C.Road)

F.C. Road is a college crowd hotspot and perhaps one of the most lively areas for shopping in Pune. The prices of clothing here are too good to be true. Earrings and bags here are to die for! Also, you can match your dresses with innumerable phone cases available here FC Road is known among Puneites as a location to find the best possible hacks for street shopping for men and women. Score shirts, skirts, t-shirts trousers, cardigans, shirts, kurtis, and various other clothing items starting from as cheap as INR 200. You will find some trendy crop tops and palazzos too along with beach shorts, sexy lingerie and summer dresses. Quaint roadside shops full of colourful slippers, canvas and Kolhapuris adorn either side of the street, all starting at INR 150. If you’re looking for affordable eye wear, head gears and makeup, this is the place to be. If you’re on FC Road early in the morning, you ABSOLUTELY have to try the bun maska chai at the 1935 Irani restaurant, Goodluck. If you’re fan of South Indian cuisine and filter coffees, nothing beats the food served at Vaishali, Roopali and Wadeshwar. If you want to try authentic Maharastrian thali, head to Shabree for a lavishing meal.

Tulsi Bagh

Tulsi Bag is in the heart of Pune and a haven for street shopping. Super famous for ready-made garments, you will find rows of shops available for your customised fittings here. This is one of the oldest markets for shopping in Pune equivalent to Delhi's Chandni Chowk. The shopping atmosphere starts at Alka Talkies square and ends near the cantonment area. Be it Jeans, blouses, shirts or accessories, all of these are accessible at great deals. Disorderly, noisy and very crowded, this market place is lined with stalls and shops selling a wide range of household items and other bric-a-brac. It's a bit of a challenge to traverse, but we suggest you do the time, you'll emerge arms laden and none-the-poorer. From jewellery and traditional Maharashtrians wares, to sarees and pearl necklaces, you’ll get it all here.

Mahatma Phule Mandai

Mahatma Phule Mandai in Shukrawaris an excellent spot for shopping for fruits and vegetables at wholesale prices. It is a place filled with street-hawkers and sellers selling on their bikes and cars and has a whopping 530 stalls The construction started in 1882 and it was completed in 1886 at a whopping cost of 2.3 lakhs, which seems nothing in today’s day and age, but it was a handsome amount 132 years ago. Today, the market has approximately 1,438 spacious fruit and vegetable stalls and it's crowded all throughout the day with people thronging to get their hands on the freshest produce. It gets supremely crowded on the weekends and holidays.

Hong Kong Lane

This super-popular street on Deccan Road is the place for shopping in Pune. The accessories like bags, books and mobile stores are what dominates this lane. Shops here are overloaded with mobile phone cases and decked with sunglasses, caps and wallets Hong Kong Lane is best known for its wide collection of phone covers. We picked two for ourselves; one was a multi-colour Aztec case and second, was a bright yellow piece with quirky illustration. The place hosts a lot of mobile covers, key chains and other small mobile accessories, at relatively cheap prices. Besides these, around the area are many shops selling women's shoes and handbags, some stores selling unisex t-shirts, some selling shawls. Overall, the area is worth a visit. You just might find a gem among all of these.

Pimpri

Pimpri is one of the best places for shopping. All the things are available in cheapest rate. Pimpri Market contains the various sections like Mobile Market, Electronics Market,Flower Market,Cloth market,Book Market,and many more.
